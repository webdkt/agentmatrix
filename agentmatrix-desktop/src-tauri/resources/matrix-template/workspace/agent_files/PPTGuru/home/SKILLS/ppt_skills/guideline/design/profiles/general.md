# General

> Applicable scenarios: Product introductions, technology showcases, general project promotions, brand introductions, knowledge sharing, versatile presentations without a specific strong style anchor.
> Style anchor: Modern tech company presentations (Apple, Google I/O, Stripe) + versatile professional templates

## Design Philosophy
- **Modern and clean**: Embrace contemporary design sensibilities — generous whitespace, bold typography, and purposeful visuals
- **Adaptable neutrality**: The general profile is a chameleon; it should feel professional without being tied to a specific industry aesthetic
- **Visual storytelling**: Even general presentations benefit from a narrative arc; use visuals to guide the audience through the story
- **Polished professionalism**: Every element should feel intentional and refined; avoid sloppy alignment, inconsistent spacing, or mismatched styles
- **Information density: Moderate (60-75%)**: Balanced density that works for both live presentations and self-guided reading

## Text-to-Visual Ratio: Flexible, Visually Engaged
- Adapt the ratio based on content type: product showcases lean visual; knowledge sharing leans textual
- Default recommended ratio: approximately 45% text + 45% visuals (images, diagrams, charts, icons) + 10% structural whitespace
- Use visuals to break up text-heavy sections; use text to anchor visual-heavy sections
- Icons and diagrams can serve as both visual interest and information carriers

## Color Guidance
- **Modern, versatile palette**: Choose a palette that feels current and professional without being tied to a specific industry cliché
- **Primary color options** (choose one that fits the presentation mood):
  - Tech/Innovation: Electric blue (#2563EB), vivid teal (#0D9488), deep violet (#7C3AED)
  - Warm/Approachable: Coral (#F97316), soft amber (#D97706), warm rose (#E11D48)
  - Cool/Professional: Slate blue (#475569), muted teal (#0F766E), steel gray (#64748B)
  - Fresh/Organic: Sage green (#15803D), sky blue (#0369A1), warm sand (#A16207)
- **Neutral foundation**: White or very light gray (#F8FAFC, #F1F5F9) backgrounds; charcoal (#1E293B, #334155) or near-black text
- **Accent discipline**: One accent color (complementary or analogous to primary) for highlights, CTAs, and emphasis; use sparingly
- **Dark mode option**: General presentations may use dark backgrounds (#0F172A, #1E293B) for dramatic effect on covers and key pages; ensure high contrast
- **Avoid**: Outdated color clichés (corporate navy + red unless brand-required), rainbow palettes, neon colors, excessive gradient use

## Font Guidance
- **Titles and display**: Modern geometric sans-serif (e.g., Poppins, Montserrat, MiSans) for a clean, contemporary feel; or elegant serif (e.g., Playfair Display, Source Han Serif) for sophisticated introductions
- **Body text**: Highly readable sans-serif (e.g., Inter, Open Sans, MiSans, Microsoft YaHei)
- **Chinese**: MiSans for modern, clean presentations; Source Han Sans for technical content; Songti for formal/traditional contexts
- Font size hierarchy should be clear and modern:
  - Cover/hero title: 48-64px (oversized typography as a design element)
  - Section/chapter titles: 36-44px
  - Page titles: 24-32px
  - Body text: 18-22px (must not go below 18px)
  - Captions, labels, metadata: 14-16px
  - Minimum font size: 12px (only for fine print, disclaimers)

## Content Page Structure
- **Flexible, content-adaptive layouts**:
  - Hero/Introduction: Large visual + bold headline + brief subtitle
  - Feature showcase: Icon + title + description (grid of 2-4 features)
  - Data/Proof: Chart or metric + interpretation text
  - Process/How it works: Step-by-step flow (numbered, with visuals)
  - Comparison: Side-by-side or grid comparison layout
  - Testimonial/Quote: Large quote + attribution + photo (optional)
  - Call-to-Action: Clean, focused page with clear next step
- **Common layouts**:
  - Full-width image + overlay text (for impact pages)
  - Two-column split (text + image, or text + chart)
  - Three-column grid (features, benefits, comparisons)
  - Central focal point (diagram, chart, or key visual centered)
  - Asymmetrical layouts (e.g., 1/3 + 2/3 split) for visual interest

## Narrative Style: Clear, Engaging, Purposeful
- Establish the purpose early: What is this presentation about and why should the audience care?
- Structure with flexibility: Problem -> Solution -> Proof -> Call to Action is a safe default, but adapt to content
- Use the "So What?" test: Every section should clearly connect to the audience's interests or needs
- Maintain consistent pacing: Alternate between information-dense pages and visually striking "breather" pages
- End with a memorable conclusion and clear next steps

## Content Expression Techniques
- **Feature grids**: 2x2 or 1x3 layouts with icon + bold title + 1-2 sentence description for each feature
- **Process flows**: Horizontal or vertical step sequences; use connecting lines, numbers, or arrows to show progression
- **Data storytelling**: Embed charts within a narrative context; don't just present data, explain its significance
- **Quote/testimonial blocks**: Large, visually distinct typography for quotes; include attribution and optional photo/logo
- **Before/After**: Side-by-side comparison layouts showing transformation or improvement
- **Icon-driven lists**: Replace bullet points with relevant icons for visual scannability
- **Image galleries**: Grid of 2-4 images with captions for portfolios, product shots, or case studies
- **Embedded media references**: Video stills with play button overlays, or QR codes linking to external content

## Image Usage Rules
**Allowed (purposeful visuals)** — Images that enhance understanding or create impact:

| Image Type | Examples | Applicable Pages |
|---------|------|---------|
| Product/service photos | Product shots, UI screenshots, service photography | Introduction, feature, showcase pages |
| Lifestyle/usage photos | People using the product, real-world application scenes | Benefits, use-case pages |
| Team/company photos | Team portraits, office photos, company events | About us, team, culture pages |
| Portfolio/work samples | Design work, project screenshots, case study imagery | Portfolio, case study pages |
| Data visualizations | Charts, graphs, infographics (prefer native chart elements) | Proof, metrics, analysis pages |
| Concept illustrations | Custom illustrations explaining concepts or processes | Explanation, how-it-works pages |

**Prohibited (low-quality or generic visuals)**:
- Overused, generic stock photos (e.g., handshake, team huddle, generic "success" imagery)
- Low-resolution or poorly cropped images
- Unrelated decorative images that do not serve the narrative
- Images with visible watermarks or copyright issues

## Decoration Guidelines (Modern and Purposeful)
| Element | Guideline |
|--------|---------|
| Rounded corners | Allowed and encouraged (8-16px) for cards, images, and containers; creates a modern, friendly feel |
| Subtle shadows | Soft, diffuse shadows (4-8px blur, very light opacity) for cards and floating elements; avoid heavy drop shadows |
| Gradients | Allowed for backgrounds, hero sections, and accent elements; keep subtle and brand-aligned |
| Icons | Solid or outline style, consistent set, monochrome or primary-colored; 24-48px depending on context |
| Decorative shapes | Abstract geometric accents (circles, lines, dots) as background texture; very low opacity |
| Overlays | Gradient overlays on images to ensure text legibility; dark or primary-colored gradients |

## Decoration Prohibitions
| Prohibited | Alternative |
|--------|---------|
| Outdated design tropes | Beveled buttons, 3D effects, heavy drop shadows, glossy reflections |
| Cluttered, cramped layouts | Maintain generous padding (24-40px) around elements and sections |
| Inconsistent visual styles | Stick to one icon style, one rounding radius, one shadow style throughout |
| Decorative elements that reduce readability | Ensure all decorative elements have sufficient contrast with text |
| Excessive animation or motion hints | Static, clean layouts that work in print and digital |

## Additional General-Specific Guidelines
- **Adapt to audience**: General presentations may serve diverse audiences (investors, customers, internal teams, students); adjust density and tone accordingly
- **Brand neutrality**: If no specific brand is being promoted, avoid colors that strongly suggest a competitor or unrelated industry
- **Cross-platform compatibility**: General decks are often shared via PDF or viewed on various devices; test readability at 100% zoom on both large and small screens
- **Modularity**: Design layouts that can be easily reordered or repurposed; general presentations often evolve over multiple iterations
- **Accessibility**: Ensure sufficient color contrast (WCAG AA standard); don't rely solely on color to convey meaning; provide text alternatives for visual elements when possible
- **Cultural sensitivity**: For international audiences, avoid culturally specific idioms, colors with negative connotations in certain regions, or imagery that may not translate well
